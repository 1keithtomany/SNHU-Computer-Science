CS-230 - Operating Platforms

Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
  The Gaming Room client was looking to create a web based version of their mobile game, Draw It or Lose It, that could be used on multiple platforms (it was only 
  available on Android). The game competes multiple teams consisting of several people going four rounds, each round lasting a minute. The game needed to run on
  multiple platforms via web-based distribution, only one instance of the game could exist at a time, and game/team names needed to be unique to allow users to check 
  whether a name is in use when choosing a team name. I think I did well with clearly articulating what the client was looking for, though I could have more detailed 
  on the design constraints.

What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
  Having to write out all of the requirements for the program helped me plan out my code, mapping out what I needed to focus on or to avoid before writing code. 

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
  I would revise the Design Constraints section by expanding on it. I would add more constrains I failed to list (such as time tracking, UI, error handling, and 
  multiplayer support) so that we could have an even clearer picture of what the program's constraints are. 

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
  When designing, I try to take a step back and put myself into their shoes. How would they react to what I've designed? Would I enjoy using it? You want the client 
  to appreciate your program to retain them as a customer.

How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
  Creating a UML was very helpful in maping out and understanding how the various classes and processes interact and will need to be implemented. Having one place, the 
  design document, to refer to for all design concerns is also quite helpful.

CS-250 - Software Development Lifecycle

1). How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
  User Stories help to elabotate end-user needs into simple statements - “As a [role], I want to [action], so that [value/outcome].” User stories serve 
  as requirements told from the perspective of the end-user. They help me view the software through the user's eyes, ensuring the final product actually 
  solves their specific pain points. They also allow me to decompose complex project requirements into small, manageable, and testable units of work. 

2). How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
  To keep from getting overwhelmed, I would break my code down into smaller, more manageable milestones. Looking ahead, I aim to refine my Agile workflow by 
  prioritizing proactive planning based on current requirements. My goal is to adopt a value-driven development mindset, ensuring that core functionality and 
  user impact always take precedence over purely aesthetic enhancements.

3). What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
  Being an effective teammate means truly embracing the collaborative nature of Agile. It requires active participation in daily standups, communicating blockers 
  immediately to minimize downtime, and keeping the team informed of both progress and setbacks. Ultimately, a good team member fosters an environment of honesty,
  offering help where possible and having the humility to ask for it when needed.
